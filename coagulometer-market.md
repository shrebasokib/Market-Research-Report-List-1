<p><ul><li>Mechanical Detection</li><li>Photo-Optical Detection</li><li>Electrochemical Detection</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The coagulometer market is segmented based on detection methods. Mechanical detection involves measuring blood viscosity and clot formation through physical movement, while photo-optical detection utilizes light transmission or reflection to assess clotting. Electrochemical detection employs electrical impedance changes to monitor the coagulation process. Other methods may include advanced technologies like ultrasonic or immunological approaches. Each type offers unique advantages for accuracy, speed, and application, catering to varying clinical needs in coagulation testing and management.</p></p>
