<p><p>The disc brakes market primarily consists of two types: Cast Iron and Carbon-Matrix Composites (CMC). Cast Iron disc brakes are widely utilized due to their durability and cost-effectiveness, suitable for standard automotive applications. In contrast, CMC disc brakes are engineered for high-performance environments, offering lightweight properties and exceptional thermal handling, making them ideal for racing and advanced automotive applications. The choice between these materials depends on performance requirements, weight considerations, and budget constraints within the braking system.</p></p>
