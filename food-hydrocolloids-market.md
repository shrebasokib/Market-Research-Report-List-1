<p><p>The food hydrocolloids market encompasses diverse types used for thickening, gelling, stabilizing, and emulsifying in food products. Agar, a vegetarian gelling agent, and alginates, derived from seaweed, are popular in desserts and dairy products. Carboxymethylcellulose and other cellulose ethers enhance texture and moisture retention. Carrageenan offers texture in dairy alternatives, while gelatin provides a traditional gelling agent from animal sources. Gellan gum creates stable gels, guar gum thickens, gum acacia acts as an emulsifier, and locust bean gum adds creaminess. Other hydrocolloids include xanthan gum and pectin, each serving unique functional roles.</p></p>
