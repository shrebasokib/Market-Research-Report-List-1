<p><p>The marine fuel cell market primarily features two types: Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC) and Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC). PEMFCs operate at low temperatures, offering rapid startup and high efficiency, making them suitable for smaller vessels and applications requiring immediate power. In contrast, SOFCs function at higher temperatures, enabling them to utilize a broader range of fuels and providing longer operational durations, which is advantageous for larger ships and long-haul maritime operations.</p></p>
