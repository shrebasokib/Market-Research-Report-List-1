<p><p>Petroleum geochemistry testing encompasses two primary market types: laboratory-based and in-field based testing. Laboratory-based testing involves collecting samples from exploration sites and analyzing them in controlled environments to provide detailed assessments of hydrocarbon potential, composition, and source. In contrast, in-field based testing utilizes portable equipment to conduct real-time analysis at exploration sites, enabling quicker decision-making. Both approaches are essential for evaluating oil and gas resources, though laboratory testing offers more comprehensive data while in-field testing provides immediate insights.</p></p>
