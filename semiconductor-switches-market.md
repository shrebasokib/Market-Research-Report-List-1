<p><p>The semiconductor switches market can be divided into AC and DC segments based on operational voltage types. AC semiconductor switches, including devices like TRIACs and thyristors, manage alternating current applications, widely used in lighting, heating, and motor control. Conversely, DC semiconductor switches, such as MOSFETs and IGBTs, are essential for direct current applications, commonly found in renewable energy systems, electric vehicles, and power supplies. Each type serves distinct roles, catering to specific needs in electrical and electronic systems.</p></p>
