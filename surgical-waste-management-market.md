<p><p>Surgical waste management encompasses methods for disposing of medical waste generated in healthcare facilities. Incineration involves burning waste at high temperatures, effectively reducing volume and destroying pathogens but requires careful emission controls. Autoclaving uses steam and pressure to sterilize waste, making it safe for disposal or recycling. Chemical disinfection employs chemicals to neutralize infections before disposal. Together, these methods aim to minimize the environmental impact while ensuring public health safety, with each technique offering unique benefits and challenges.</p></p>
